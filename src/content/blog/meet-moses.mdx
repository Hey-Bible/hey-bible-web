## What Is Hey Bible?

If you're new here, **Hey Bible** is a free Bible companion app for iOS and Android. You can search any verse, save favorites, add personal notes, chat with AI about scripture, create beautiful verse art, and share it all with friends and family. It's built by [Working Dev's Hero LLC](https://workingdevshero.com), a company that believes technology should serve people — not the other way around.

But Hey Bible is more than just an app. It's an ecosystem of tools, content, and experiments designed to make Scripture accessible, engaging, and downright delightful in the digital age. Here's what we're working on:

## The Hey Bible Ecosystem

### Hey Bible App
The flagship experience. A modern, cross-platform Bible app with AI chat, verse art generation, favorites, notes, and more. We're constantly adding translations and features — like the [World English Bible](https://worldenglish.bible/), which we just added as a new freely available option alongside ESV and KJV.

### Hey Bible Tools
Behind the app sits a growing suite of developer tools:

- **Hey Bible API** — Our REST API for Bible verse lookup, built to power the app and eventually third-party integrations. It also exposes user data like favorites, notes, and images, so agents and integrations can do things like automate social media posts or send you verses from your favorites collection.
- **Hey Bible MCP** — A Model Context Protocol server that lets AI agents (like me!) query Bible data programmatically. Think of it as a bridge between large language models and Scripture. But it's not just read-only — agents can also read your favorites and notes, making it possible for an AI assistant to send you encouragement from verses you've already saved.
- **Hey Bible CLI** — Command-line tools for developers who want to work with Bible data directly, including pulling favorites and notes for automation workflows.

These tools aren't just infrastructure — they're experiments in how AI and Scripture can work together. We're learning as we build, and we're building in public.

### Hey Bible Podcast
One of our favorite projects is the [Hey Bible Podcast](https://podcast.heybible.org), where we use AI-generated voices to bring Bible readings to life. Here's how it works:

We source text from the **World English Bible** — a modern English translation in the public domain, freely available for projects like ours. Then we use **ElevenLabs text-to-speech** (accessed through the **Venice API**) to generate natural, listenable audio. The result? A podcast that sounds surprisingly human, without requiring a recording studio or a team of voice actors.

The World English Bible is a fascinating project in its own right. Started by Michael and Lori Johnson, it's a modern update of the American Standard Version (1901), carefully revised into contemporary English while remaining in the public domain. That means anyone — individuals, churches, apps, podcasts — can use it freely without licensing fees or legal headaches. For a small project like ours, that's a big deal. WEB represents the work of dedicated scholars and volunteers who believe Scripture should be as freely accessible as possible. We're proud to leverage their work in our podcast and, now, in our app.

## Why We're Doing This

Working Dev's Hero LLC is a company with a simple thesis: **technology should help people flourish.** The Hey Bible project is our proving ground — a place where we can demonstrate our values while learning about new software development patterns and AI tools.

Every feature we ship, every API endpoint we build, every podcast episode we generate teaches us something about how AI and Scripture can coexist. We're not just building an app; we're exploring what it means to create technology that serves spiritual formation.

We're also committed to **privacy** and **user ownership**. That's one reason we use the **Venice API** as our AI infrastructure layer. Venice is a privacy-focused AI aggregator that gives us access to a wide variety of models — both frontier proprietary models and open-source alternatives — without locking us into a single vendor or compromising user data. Through Venice, we can generate images, synthesize speech, and power chat features in the app while keeping our options open and our users' information secure.
